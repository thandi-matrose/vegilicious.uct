RE: The Crisis at UCT


We, as Vegilicious, would like to express our solidarity with the efforts made by the SRC and various faculty councils to address the Housing Crisis and Fee Blocks present for many students at UCT. The demands made, we find, are entirely reasonable and the lack of cooperation by UCT Executive is profoundly disappointing to say the least.

To be denied housing is to be denied a basic recognition of humanity. As a society, we aim to make it easier for students to be more conscientious about what we eat but, whatâ€™s happening right in front of our eyes, what the crisis at UCT has resulted in, is our fellow students being denied registration and housing, they do not have mere access to food, among many other basic necessities.
The reality of this is disturbing.

We concur with the SRC in that these issues have been and are historic, recurring and, after years upon years, unsurprising. This fact should fill every person with indignation.
We, as Vegilicious, urge all of our society members to keep themselves and their communities informed. Care for the environment, care for animals, care for your body, care for your spirit: these are the reasons we are vegan, vegetarian, plant-based or follow religious dietary requirements, and so we implore you to follow your convictions and participate in restoring dignity to our fellow students.
